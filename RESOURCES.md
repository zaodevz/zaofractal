### Academic / Theoretical Sources

| Person | Field | Why cited |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| **James Fishkin** (Stanford) | Deliberative Democracy Lab founder | Deliberative Polling methodology - the empirical proof that structured small-group deliberation produces measurably different decisions than mass voting |
| **Helene Landemore** (Yale) | Open Democracy theorist | Argues cognitive diversity beats expert aggregation. Cited in ZAO's theory chapter. |
| **Jurgen Habermas** (Frankfurt School) | Communicative action theorist | The four validity claims - comprehensibility, truth, sincerity, rightness - that underpin Respect Game presentations |
| **Yves Sintomer + Julien Talpin** | Contemporary sortition scholars | The European deliberative-wave literature, Ireland + France case studies |
| **Hugo Mercier + Dan Sperber** | The Enigma of Reason (2017) | Argumentative theory of reason - why human reasoning evolved for small-group debate |
| **Cass Sunstein** | Infotopia (2006), Why Societies Need Dissent (2003) | The case AGAINST consensus uniformity, the structural value of dissent |
| **Elinor Ostrom** | Governing the Commons (1990) | 8 design principles for common-pool resource governance - directly applicable to $ZAO Respect as a common-pool resource |
| **James Surowiecki** | The Wisdom of Crowds (2004) | Why diverse independent decision-makers beat experts under specific conditions - which Fractal meets, token-voting does not |
| **Bernard Manin** | The Principles of Representative Government (1997) | Argues elections were adopted as an ANTI-democratic mechanism vs sortition |
| **Navajas, Niella et al.** | Nature Human Behaviour, 2018 | Empirical paper - "aggregated knowledge from a small number of debates outperforms the wisdom of large crowds" |
